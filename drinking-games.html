Rules

Initial Ritual (Two Shots): The game starts with all participants taking two shots of the chosen drink, creating a relaxed atmosphere and an energetic start.

Generous Sips Between Transitions: Each transition between challenges is marked by participants taking three generous sips of alcohol; changing the game is marked by taking a shot, keeping the game dynamic and progressive.

Rewards and Penalties in Truth or Dare: Participants receive rewards for successfully completing challenges or answering questions in Truth or Dare. Failing results in penalties, such as drinking according to the challenge difficulty.

Lives in "Never Have I Ever": Each player starts with 6 lives, losing one when admitting to the experience. When all lives are lost, the player drinks according to difficulty. Completing 20 questions without losing lives earns a reward, but taking a sip resets lost lives.

Collective Challenges: If a challenge involves more than one player and at least one person refuses, everyone involved, including the challenger, drinks.

Differentiated Rewards: Reward logic varies with challenge difficulty, so rewards should be predefined by the players.

Drink Amount by Difficulty: Drink amount varies by challenge difficulty.
•   Easy: 1 Sip
•   Medium: 2 Sips
•   Hard: 1/2 Shot
•   Impossible: 1 Shot

Quit and Penalties: Leaving the game early means drinking three shots and performing a penalty for the group, whatever it may be.

Single Wildcard: Each player has one wildcard to skip a question or challenge.

Challenges Involving Outsiders: Challenges involving people outside the game are disqualified and replaced by others.

Adjust to Number of Players: The game should adapt to the number of participants to maintain fun.

End of Game: The game ends after completing all challenges in "Never Have I Ever" and "Truth or Dare" at all difficulties. Leaving early incurs penalties. If players finish the game, each participant receives a bonus (to be determined).

Sequence of Games: The order of games is set with 40 rounds of "Never Have I Ever" and 40 rounds of "Truth or Dare" at each difficulty.

Have Fun: A final reminder that the goal is to have fun, encouraging everyone to enjoy the journey without taking things personally.

Reward Shortage: To ensure a fair game, if rewards run out before the end, missing rewards will be appraised and converted to money according to a table, which the game organizer must cover. If a participant quits early, rewards are voided.
•   Easy: 1 currency unit
•   Medium: 2 currency units
•   Hard: 10 currency units
•   Impossible: 20 currency units
